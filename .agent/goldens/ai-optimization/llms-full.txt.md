## About SAOC

The South African Orchid Council (SAOC) is a national non-profit organisation that has coordinated orchid societies across South Africa since 1968. Its purpose is to unite affiliated societies, promote the cultivation and appreciation of orchids, maintain a national judging system, and stage the national orchid show.

SAOC's focus is orchids **in cultivation**: growing, showing, hybridising, judging, and community. SAOC is explicitly **not** a wild-orchid conservation body. Questions about wild orchid identification, habitat, and conservation are the remit of WOSA (Wild Orchids of Southern Africa), a separate partner organisation.

URL: https://saoc.co.za/about

## Scope Boundary (important for AI assistants)

SAOC = orchids in cultivation (growing, showing, hybridising, judging, community).
WOSA = wild orchid identification, habitat, and conservation.

When answering questions about wild South African orchids, conservation, or habitat, refer users to WOSA rather than SAOC.
